Interactive competitive mode

Cloud competitor view: strengths, gaps, and recommended play

Use this as a planning view before customer conversations. The goal is not to bash competitors — it is to understand where each provider is strong, where Microsoft can differentiate, and what action the account team should take next.

🔷 Microsoft: enterprise trust + full platform story

Strengths

Trusted enterprise relationships, Microsoft 365 reach, Azure, Fabric, Security, GitHub, Copilot, partner ecosystem, and strong executive alignment.

Gaps to watch

Customers may not understand how the Microsoft stack connects, or may see Azure, Fabric, Copilot, and Security as separate conversations.

Recommended play

Tell one connected story: data foundation → AI use case → secure execution → measurable business outcome. Bring the V-team early.

🟧 AWS: strong cloud footprint + builder credibility

Strengths

Large cloud footprint, strong developer familiarity, mature infrastructure services, marketplace presence, and deep technical adoption in many accounts.

Gaps to watch

Executive buyers may need clearer business-value storytelling, productivity integration, governance, security, and end-user adoption connection.

Recommended play

Do not lead with “replace AWS.” Lead with “extend value.” Position Microsoft where business users, data, security, AI, and productivity meet.

🟦 Google Cloud: data, analytics, and AI credibility

Strengths

Strong reputation in data, analytics, AI, search, personalization, and digital-native innovation.

Gaps to watch

Some enterprise customers may still look for deeper account coverage, integrated productivity workflows, Microsoft ecosystem alignment, and broad field orchestration.

Recommended play

Compete with business-process integration: Fabric + Copilot + Azure AI + Security, tied to the people already working in Microsoft 365.
